Humanization System
8 dimensions.
Applied simultaneously.

Every output is scored and tuned across all eight human-writing characteristics in a single pass. Not one at a time — all at once, calibrated for your chosen mode.

01
Predictability & Burstiness
Humans are random. We inject controlled chaos — short punches, 40-word expansions, abrupt stops — that detection models can't pattern-match.
02
Sentence Variation
No two consecutive sentences share grammatical structure. Subject-first, clause-first, question, fragment, conjunction-start — rotated by algorithm.
03
Mixed Vocabulary
Sophisticated terms followed immediately by plain everyday language. Intentionally uneven register. Real writers never maintain perfect vocabulary consistency.
04
Tone & Voice
Slight personality leaks through. Hedging, mild opinions, rhetorical questions. AI makes everything neutral — we break that deliberately.
05
Grammar Imperfection
Natural comma splices, deliberate fragments, em-dash thought breaks. Real writers are not perfect — and AI detectors have learned this signal.
06
Non-Repetitive Patterns
Never the same opener twice. No Furthermore / Moreover / Additionally chains. Each paragraph has a structurally different feel.
07
Specificity
No vague claims without a concrete anchor. Mild observations surface naturally. Never broad, never generic — always anchored to something real.
08
Tone Shifts
Some sentences are stronger. Some paragraphs more casual. Uneven quality is a human signal — we engineer it deliberately, not accidentally.

Signature Feature

Research Paper Mode.
Written like a real researcher.

We analysed 100+ published papers across computational linguistics, cognitive science, social science, and applied mathematics. The Research Paper mode internalises how real academics actually write — not how AI imitates them.

§1
Academic Burstiness
Dense 45-word methodological sentences broken by blunt 9-word claims. The rhythm of real papers — not AI uniformity.
§2
Epistemic Hedging
"appears to", "is broadly consistent with", "warrants careful consideration" — the precise hedging language of peer-reviewed science.
§3
Section-Level Voice
Intro is declarative. Methods are technical. Discussion is reflective. Conclusion is honest about limits. Each section sounds different — by design.
§4
Academic Personality
"The data compel a different reading." Real researchers have a point of view. We carry that into your paper without fabricating anything.
§5
Zero Fabrication
We never invent statistics, citations, or data. Every number and entity from your original is preserved exactly as written.

Abstract
This paper examines the relationship between urban green space density and self-reported wellbeing in metropolitan populations. Drawing on survey data from 3,200 participants across four cities, we find that proximity to parks within 400 metres of residence is associated with a 12.4% increase in reported life satisfaction — a finding broadly consistent with prior literature, though complicated by substantial variation across income quartiles.
Discussion
The results warrant caution. Self-reported wellbeing is notoriously susceptible to reference-group effects and recall bias. Still — and this is worth noting — the pattern holds across three of four sites. What the data do not resolve is whether green space proximity causes improved wellbeing, or whether higher-wellbeing individuals are simply more likely to reside near parks. That selection problem, inter alia, remains unresolved in the broader literature.
Conclusion
We argue that urban planning frameworks should treat green space as a wellbeing infrastructure priority, not merely an aesthetic one. The evidence is imperfect — as it almost always is — but the direction is clear enough to act on, carefully.
